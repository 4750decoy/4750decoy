Rescue Drones

Rescue Drones are designed to provide immediate supports to people who are lost in the backcountry. The app comes with the tablet in the drone and users can select specific functions from medical, call, GPS, and many other features.

Some Decision

The Very Start

I really think of drones as tools for transport, delivery, and monitor. I started this project thinking about how to make apps for city delivery. However, after checking all sorts of regulations, i realised that drones are impossible in cities. In most cases, drones are not allowed for personal uses since drones are hard to controll (which may affect public transportation, interfere the public, and so on), easy to access (people can buy drones easily), not regulated or monitored (criminals can use it to achieve multiple goals).

Knowing these information, my team starts thinking about how to apply drones in the wild area while everyone could use it. There our idea pops up, a rescue drone that helps to locate the lost and provide them proper cares.

Functionality

Carring all sorts of supports is our first idea. It is really hard to filt things out, and we just put all staffs in it at the very begining. We haved check the current drone delivery company (JD), and we have regulated our carriages according to their experience.

Reflection

Research

Doing research is actually a very serious part in design process. Sometimes, the research result will be against what you want to design. However, research can really open up the topic. Like the one i am doing. I once thought people are getting lost because they do not have tools (phones) to communicate with the outside (GPS), but the research tells me that in most cases, people are getting lost because they deny to admit they are lost at the first place. Design is not only a way to solve problem, it is also a way to understand problem.

User Test

Although we didn't do so well at this section, but some other teams' user tests are really astonishing. User tests should be where real potential users have been tested. Most of our tested users are not our potential users. It is important to gather data from the correct samples. In doing so, the conclusion will be closer to the reallity.
